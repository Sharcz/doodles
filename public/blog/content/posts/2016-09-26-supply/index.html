Supply

Arguments about the adequacy of Vancouver’s housing supply have been making the rounds again lately, but I have not found any arguments or analysis that I found sufficiently accurate or thorough. It’s an important question, so Fernando, Bryn and I got together to brainstorm how to measure this.

The first step is to estimate supply over time. Then we break down supply into different types and follow it down the road of getting sold, getting occupied by households and translate that into people to match that up to population growth.

Next up is to find some estimates of demand pressure or pent-up demand and see how that matches up against

We are in a situation where rental supply in Metro Vancouver is extremely constrained with the latest vacancy rate of 0.8%. And everyone seems to agree that we have a purpose-built rental supply shortage.

Consequently, the only point of discussion is the owner market. Of course the two are connected. If the rental market is squeezed, more people get pushed into home ownership and might take on mortgages that are larger than they are comfortable with. And vice versa.
